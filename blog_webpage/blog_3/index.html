How to Design a Good PowerPoint for Research

Author: Sam Moore

It is common in a scientific field for you to be asked to showcase your research as part of a talk, even as an undergraduate. I personally have been asked to do this when presenting my summer research, and also as a part of my undergraduate thesis defense. More commonly, powerpoints are used as part of colloquiums as a visual support for the content being presented. As such, here are some tips for giving a successful talk:

The PowerPoint:

Ensure that you are always concise when using words on a PowerPoint slide. If a slide is too cluttered it can confuse people and deter their interest. Additionally the golden rule of PowerPoint presentations is that the PowerPoint should complement the spoken element of the talk: do not read verbatim from your PowerPoint slides!

It is considered polite to include a slide at the end of the PowerPoint giving acknowledgements and citations.
